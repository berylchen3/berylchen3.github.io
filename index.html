Research Interests & Vision

Recent advances in Vision-Language-Action models, such as OpenVLA, have demonstrated the promise of integrating internet-scale pre-training with robotic control, enabling impressive task-level generalization across diverse instructions and environments. However, a key limitation remains in their ability to perform high-frequency, dexterous manipulation, where precise, continuous control is essential.

In contrast, diffusion-based policies have shown strong performance in generating smooth, physically grounded action trajectories, but typically lack semantic grounding and cross-task generalization. I am interested in bridging this gap by exploring hybrid architectures that combine the strengths of both paradigms.

My future research goal is to investigate how rich semantic representations learned by large VLA models can condition diffusion-based policy heads for low-level control. By moving beyond discrete, token-based action prediction and toward continuous, distributional motion generation, I aim to develop generalist robotic policies that retain strong language grounding while achieving high-fidelity, dynamically consistent manipulation.
